The Mechanics

How a flip loan actually works

The part most lenders don't explain until closing — here it is up front.

01 · THE DEAL

We underwrite the asset

Three numbers drive everything: your purchase price, your rehab budget, and the ARV — the After-Repair Value, set by what comparable renovated homes actually sold for near your property. Strong comps mean stronger terms.

02 · THE SCOPE

Your scope of work sets your ARV

A clear scope of work (SOW) — line-item renovation budget and plan — lets us compare your property against homes rehabbed to the same standard. Better scope, better valuation, more leverage.

03 · THE DRAWS

Rehab funded in stages

Construction funds release in draws as work completes: finish a phase, quick inspection verifies it, next draw releases. Your budget stays covered, your crews stay moving, and nobody fronts the whole rehab.

04 · THE EXIT

Sell or refinance

Interest-only payments during the project keep carry costs down. Exit by selling the finished flip — or roll it into a DSCR rental loan with us and hold it.
